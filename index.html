How the slot cost is calculated
Slot cost = (Charter hire + Bunker + Port charges + Misc) ÷ effective capacity.
Bunker = Σ (fuel MT × price) across LSFO, HSFO and MGO. Charter hire = daily rate × round-trip days.

Market vs Daily basis (on the Vessel Slot Cost page): Market (Weekly PL) uses the full weekly charter cost; Daily (Adjusted PL) uses the adjusted daily-equivalent rate. Same formula, different charter input — both reconcile to the source workbook.

Monsoon scenarios (Bunker History) reflect higher fuel burn and longer round-trip days during monsoon season versus the no-monsoon baseline.

Per FEU in the Excel summary = per-TEU slot cost × 2 (one FEU = two TEU).

How contribution margin is defined
Contribution = Total cargo revenue − Total cargo cost, per booking (B/L), as computed in the source workbook (CBP logic).

Per-unit margin for a port-pair × size cell = Σ contribution ÷ Σ units, where units = number of 20′ containers (for the 20′ bucket) or 40′ containers (for the 40′ bucket).

Size type comes from the booking's container counts: a B/L with only 20′ units goes to the 20′ bucket, only 40′ to the 40′ bucket. Mixed B/Ls (both 20′ and 40′) are excluded.

Cost basis: Incl. doc costs uses Total Contribution; Excl. doc costs strips document-related revenue and cost. Port pair: POL→POD is the ocean leg; POR→DEL is door-to-door origin/destination.

Search a port pair to understand its cost per unit and what drives it. Each card shows the cost-per-unit broken into its seven components (agent, terminal, haulage, slot, fixed), worked back from revenue − contribution. Mixed 20′/40′ B/Ls are excluded. Source: Contribution_by_Booking.xlsx.
How cost per unit is derived
Total cost = Total revenue − Contribution (per the source workbook). It equals the sum of seven components: Agent Commission, Laden Terminal, Laden Haulage, Internal Slot Cost, Empty Terminal, Empty Haulage and Container Fixed Cost.

Cost / unit for a port-pair card = Σ cost ÷ Σ units (units = 20′ or 40′ container count depending on the size filter). Each component is shown per unit and as a % of total cost.

Incl. vs excl. doc costs switches between the two cost bases; POL→POD is the ocean leg, POR→DEL door-to-door.
